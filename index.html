Biography

I am conducting interdisciplinary research to address how vegetation responds to climate variability and long-term change and ecosystem recovery and resilience under extreme events. I combine multiple datasets, including eddy-covariance, remote sensing, and tree ring with Earth system models, employing tools from physics-based theories to machine learning techniques to gain a mechanistic of biological process to inform projections under future climate change scenarios better.

Interests

The fundamental questions I am askin​g are:

  • How is global vegetation changing, and what are the drivers of global vegetation change?
  • How do drought and climate change affect ecosystems, and how do ecosystem resistance and resilience change to droughts in a warming world?
  • What are the thresholds of water use and water stress in key climate sensitive biomes (e.g., tropical/dry transition forests, boreal forests) and does this threshold change in warming world?
  • How does drought legacy differ in leaf, canopy, tree rings and whole ecosystems by leveraging multi-source datasets?

Vegetation Resilience in Drylands is under high risks to hot droughts

Phenology effects played a import role to vegetation drought recovery. Using multiple remotely-sensed datasets, we found that more than half of ecosystems fail to recover from extreme droughts within a single growing-season. Earlier spring phenology in the drought year slowed drought recovery when extreme droughts occurred in mid-growing-season.
